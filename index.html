Why Star Wars?

I was born about 5 months before The Phantom Menace came out, so I quite literally grew up with Star Wars. My father made sure I was an Original Trilogy fan.

One of my fondest memories growing up was my friend down the street bringing his original Xbox to my Grandma's house so we could play Battlefront split screen on a tiny 12 inch TV in the garage. I'd watch The Clone Wars on TV, I'd play with the LEGO sets, I'd rewatch the movies over and over. I was obsessed.

Over the years I'd still watch the movies or play the games, but I slowly fell out of love with the franchise. Finally, in 2015 Disney put out a new film. My dad decided it was a good idea to bring his 16 year old son to see the movie at 10am on New Year's Day.

In October 2017, I joined the US military. Quickly, I found myself overseas by May 2018. During the countless hours on night shift in the middle of the open ocean, my buddies and I would slowly watch through all the movies and shows. And just like that, my love for Star Wars was back.

I'm not ashamed to say I have spent probably a couple thousand dollars and hours on watching the movies and the shows, reading the books, playing the games, and buying the LEGO sets.

I blame my father for this addiction, and I wouldn't have it any other way.
